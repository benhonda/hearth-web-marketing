# Impeccable — Design Context for Hearth Press

Scope: marketing web (hearth.press), iOS app, and the printed hardcover cookbook. All three share one design system — "the book is the app made physical." Any design skill invoked in this repo should treat these three surfaces as one object.

Source of truth for specifics (palette, type scale, component tokens, motion) is `/workspace/DESIGN.md`. This file captures the *why* and the non-obvious context that design skills need up front.

## Design Context

### Users

Home cooks. Archetype: Ben's girlfriend — not chefs, not food creators, not professionals. She saves recipes from TikTok, Instagram, and web pages day-to-day, and occasionally wants a permanent artifact (a hardcover cookbook) for an occasion (Mother's Day, wedding, anniversary, a phase of life). Context: phone in hand, kitchen or couch, unhurried. Not under time pressure. Not tracking macros. Not meal-planning.

Job to be done: *"Save every recipe I love, let it live beautifully, and — when the moment calls for it — turn it into a real book."*

Emotional goals: confidence, calm, quiet pride in the object. Never urgency, never gamified delight, never FOMO.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **literary, hand-made, confident**.

Voice: a boutique publishing house, not a SaaS app. Factual where factual serves ("50 min · Serves 4"). Warmth from phrasing, never from emoji or exclamation marks. Em-middot separators. No tutorials, no tooltips, no walkthroughs — a beautiful object doesn't need instructions.

References: Chez Panisse monograph. Phaidon cookbook. Foil-stamped hardcover in a bookshop window. A cloth-bound library reference in candlelight.

Anti-references: modern SaaS dashboards. Food-blog farmhouse aesthetic. Rustic-chic. Notion/Linear tech-UI. Anything neon, glossy, bouncy, or emoji-forward. Paprika. Do not become Paprika.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Editorial · cookbook · publishing-house.** Warm, unhurried, foil-stamped, candlelit.

Dual theme, not one or the other:
- **Dark surfaces = Deep Conifer sage hardcover** with Antique Foil gold stamping. Used for covers, hero sections, final CTA bands — anything that should read as "the cover of a book."
- **Light surfaces = White Dove cream page interior** with Deep Ochre ink. Used for body content, cards, reading surfaces — anything that should read as "a page inside the book."

The two are **two sides of the same object**, not competing themes. Pick the one that matches the metaphor of the surface you're designing.

Zero saturated accents. No blue, no red, no purple. The chromatic range is sage ↔ cream ↔ gold/ochre ↔ charcoal — every color must feel like it could have been mixed from ink, paper, and gold leaf.

Corners: disciplined 2px radius (the corner of a well-cut book page). Pills (999px) exist only on buttons as an intentional "this is tappable" signal. Nothing else.

Motion: restrained. ~280ms ease on state changes. No springs, no bounces, no overshoot. The chevron-translate 3px on T2 buttons is the entire scope of "delight" permitted. Users shouldn't consciously notice motion — that's the point.

### Typography — Locked Trio (Impeccable Reflex-List Exception)

Hearth uses **Marcellus (wordmark only), Lora (display/italic), DM Sans (body/UI)**.

Lora and DM Sans appear on impeccable's `reflex_fonts_to_reject` list. This is an **explicit, documented exception** — confirmed by the founder. The choices are principled, not reflex:

- **Marcellus** is reserved exclusively for the "HEARTH" wordmark (no italic, no alternate weights, appears nowhere else). Justified by publishing-house wordmark tradition.
- **Lora** was chosen as a transitional book serif — warm, editorial, slightly bookish without feeling antique. It carries *all* italic jobs (Marcellus has no italic). Hierarchy comes from size and color; **never** from bold weight.
- **DM Sans** is the quiet neutral — it lets the serifs do the emotional work. Used for small-caps eyebrows (0.22–0.30em tracking), body, and UI chrome.

Do not switch these without the founder's approval. When skills suggest alternative fonts, they will be refused.

### Design Principles

1. **Book spine test.** Every decision must look right on a hardcover spine. If it would embarrass the object in a bookshop, it fails.
2. **App ≡ Book.** The screen and the page share one typographic system. A recipe on iPhone and a recipe in the printed cookbook should feel like the same artifact in two media.
3. **Craft over features.** Premium feeling comes from small unexpected typographic moments (the italic Roman-numeral step marker, the em-dash folio `— 47 —`, the 0.28em small-caps eyebrow), not from features or delight effects.
4. **Empty state is a designed object, not a to-do.** Never "nothing here yet." Always typographic, always confident, always feels intentional.
5. **Typography carries emotion; photography is evidence.** Covers, chapter openers, and cookbook interiors are typographic only. Photography lives in exactly two places: recipe-detail heroes (with gradient scrim) and small 32–40px square library thumbnails. Honest, imperfect, true-to-phone — no AI enhancement, no filters that fake moodiness.
6. **Corners, pills, and weight are load-bearing signals.** 2px radius = printed page. Pill = tappable button. Lora-italic = emotion/meta. DM Sans small-caps-wide-tracked = cookbook chapter mark. Breaking these breaks the system.

### Accessibility Target

**WCAG AA across the board, AAA where the palette allows.** AA-large is permitted only as an explicit exception for tertiary text on cream (Oat #7C7465 at 4.04:1) — never for primary or secondary content, never on any interactive element.

Every color pairing in `DESIGN.md` lists its measured contrast ratio. New pairings must be measured and documented before they ship. `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` must disable the ~280ms transitions everywhere; since motion is already minimal, reduced-motion should feel like the same interface with instant state changes.

### What this means for every design skill

- Do NOT suggest Inter, Fraunces, Playfair, or any of the usual font-swaps. The type trio is locked.
- Do NOT introduce saturated accent colors. Not even "just for error states" — Alert Rust (#A55C2B) is the only warm exception and is reserved for destructive indicators.
- Do NOT suggest dark mode as an alternative to light mode, or vice versa. Both exist intentionally on different surfaces. The question is always "is this a cover/hero (sage) or an interior page (cream)?"
- Do NOT add springy, bouncy, or orchestrated motion. Ambient stillness is the aesthetic.
- DO reach for editorial layout moves: small-caps eyebrows with wide tracking, 44×1.5px Deep-Ochre rules, italic Roman-numeral lists, `— 47 —` folios, 52–58ch body widths, chapter-mark rhythm.
- DO treat `DESIGN.md` as authoritative for tokens. If a skill output contradicts it, the skill is wrong.
