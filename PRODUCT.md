# Hearth

**A recipe app that is also a publishing house.**

## What

Mobile app where home cooks save and organize recipes, and turn them into premium hardcover cookbooks whenever the occasion calls for one.

## Who

Home cooks. Archetype: my girlfriend — not chefs, not food creators, not professionals. She saves recipes from TikTok, Instagram, and web pages day-to-day, and occasionally wants a permanent artifact (a hardcover cookbook) for an occasion: Mother's Day, wedding, anniversary, a phase of life.

Context of use: phone in hand, kitchen or couch, unhurried. Not under time pressure. Not tracking macros. Not meal-planning.

Job to be done: *"Save every recipe I love, let it live beautifully, and — when the moment calls for it — turn it into a real book."*

Emotional goals: **confidence, calm, quiet pride in the object.** Never urgency, never gamified delight, never FOMO.

## Positioning

Production quality of a published cookbook. Content that is personal and yours. App and book share one design system — the book is the app made physical.

Not a scrapbook. Not a photo book. A *cookbook* that belongs on a coffee table, assembled from recipes you actually make.

## Brand

- **Mark**: `HEARTH` (gold serif caps — spine, app icon, wordmark)
- **Company**: Hearth Press
- **Domain**: hearth.press
- **Tagline (short)**: *A beautiful home for your recipes.*
- **Tagline (long)**: *A beautiful home for your recipes — and a beautiful cookbook whenever you want one.*
- **Conversion CTA**: *Turn your recipes into a real cookbook.*

## Voice

**Three words: literary · hand-made · confident.**

- A boutique publishing house, not a SaaS app.
- Factual where factual serves — "50 min · Serves 4" stays that way, with the em-middot separator.
- Warmth from phrasing, never from emoji or exclamation marks.
- No tutorials, no tooltips, no walkthroughs. A beautiful object doesn't need instructions.
- Conversational only where it helps: empty states, toasts after non-trivial actions, conversion moments. Confirm with a noun: *"Saved — it's in your library."* Never *"Great job!"*.

- Phaidon cookbook
- Foil-stamped hardcover in a bookshop window
- Cloth-bound library reference under candlelight

**Anti (what Hearth must not be):**

- Anything neon, glossy, bouncy, or emoji-forward
- Paprika. Do not become Paprika.

## Wedge

Every recipe saved is already book-worthy. Competitors are either utilitarian recipe managers (Paprika, Whisk) or blank-canvas print tools (Shutterfly, Blurb). Hearth is neither — it's a continuous library designed as a cookbook from day one.

## Moat

- Design system culture (app ≡ book, visually)
- Print fulfillment wired directly into the app
- TikTok/Instagram → structured recipe import quality
- Brand as a boutique publishing house

## Product model

- **Library**: Apple Music–style continuous chronological list
- **Collections**: Opt-in, created manually or automatically at book-creation time
- **Save flow**: Frictionless — zero organization overhead
- **Book flow**: Smart pre-selected starting point, never a blank canvas
- **Book triggers**: Multi-occasion (Mother's Day, wedding, phase, milestone), not forced calendar

## Economics

- Free app. Revenue on printed book only.
- Pricing ladder: $99 / $119 / $139 for 40 / 60 / 80 pages (8×10 hardcover, premium paper)
- Target 30% gross margin. Blurb as V1 supplier.
- Side project. Validated when girlfriend loves it.

## Load-bearing principles

1. **Typography continuity.** App and book share one font system.
2. **No tutorials, tooltips, or walkthroughs.** A beautiful object doesn't need instructions.
3. **Empty state is a designed object, not a to-do.**
4. **Craft moments over features.** Premium feeling = small unexpected touches.
5. **First import is the hero flow.** Screenshot-worthy.
6. **Book spine test.** Every design decision must look right on a hardcover spine.

## What Hearth is NOT

- Not a meal planner
- Not a shopping list app
- Not a nutrition tracker
- Not a social/sharing platform
- Not Paprika. Do not become Paprika.

## Validation

### Before any app code: the concierge cookbook

Manually design a cookbook from ~25–30 of her favorite recipes in Figma. Order from Blurb. Wrap it. Give it to her.

- Watch her reaction. Watch her mom's, her sister's, her friends' reactions.
- This single test answers more than three months of app development can.
- Cost: ~$80 and a weekend.

If the object in her hands doesn't land emotionally, no app will rescue the product. Iterate the template until the concierge cookbook works.

### The validation ladder (post V1)

Define signals in advance. Re-read before each check-in.

| Stage | Testing | Continue signal | Redirect signal |
| ----- | ------- | --------------- | --------------- |
| **1. Concierge cookbook** (pre-build) | Does the object land? | She's emotional. Friends ask "can you make me one?" | She's polite. No organic external pull. |
| **2. V1 daily-use** (4–6 weeks post-launch) | Does the app earn a place in her routine? | Reaches for Hearth unprompted. 20+ recipes saved organically. | Drifts back to screenshots / Paprika / Notes. |
| **3. V1 full loop** | Does the app's output match the concierge experience? | Orders a book, loves the result as much as the concierge version. | Abandons the book flow, or the result disappoints. |
| **4. Beyond her** (3–6 months post-launch) | Does anyone else want this? | 2+ unpaid (non-girlfriend) orders via organic word-of-mouth. | No external pull, even when she shows friends. |

### Forcing function

- **Check-in date**: 6 months after V1 ships. Honest evaluation against the ladder above.
- **Don't move goalposts.** These signals are written down now. Future-you doesn't get to redefine them.
- **Redirect options (decide before, not after)**:
  - Keep it as a personal tool for her alone.
  - Rethink the wedge.
  - Wind it down.

Deciding these options in advance prevents sunk-cost reasoning later.
